Bathroom Tile Trends for 2025

Home / Blog / Bathroom Tile Trends
February 1, 2025

Bathroom design is evolving, and tile choices are at the heart of every renovation. Whether you're planning a full bathroom remodel or a simple refresh, the right tile can transform your space from ordinary to extraordinary.

Here are the top bathroom tile trends we're seeing across Toronto and the GTA in 2025.

1. Large-Format Tiles

Oversized tiles — 24x48 inches and larger — continue to dominate modern bathroom design. Larger tiles mean fewer grout lines, creating a cleaner, more spacious look. They work beautifully on both floors and walls, especially in smaller bathrooms where visual continuity helps the space feel bigger.

Porcelain is the go-to material for large-format tiles thanks to its durability, low water absorption, and wide range of finishes that mimic natural stone, concrete, and wood.

2. Textured and 3D Tiles

Flat surfaces are giving way to tiles with depth and dimension. Textured tiles — such as fluted, ribbed, and wave patterns — add visual interest and a tactile quality to accent walls and shower niches. These tiles catch light in interesting ways, creating subtle shadows and movement throughout the day.

They're especially popular as feature walls behind vanities or as shower accent strips paired with clean, flat tiles elsewhere.

3. Warm Earth Tones

The cool gray palette that dominated the past decade is being replaced by warmer, more organic tones. Think terracotta, sand, warm beige, olive green, and soft clay. These earthy colours create a calming, spa-like atmosphere that feels both modern and timeless.

Pairing warm-toned tiles with matte black or brushed gold fixtures creates a sophisticated, grounded look that's becoming a GTA homeowner favourite.

4. Zellige and Handmade-Look Tiles

Zellige — a traditional Moroccan glazed tile — has become one of the hottest bathroom tile choices. Each tile has slight variations in colour, texture, and surface, giving walls a rich, artisanal character that mass-produced tiles can't replicate.

While authentic Zellige tiles can be expensive, many manufacturers now produce high-quality lookalikes in porcelain and ceramic that capture the handmade aesthetic at a more accessible price point.

5. Floor-to-Ceiling Tiling

Gone are the days of tiling only halfway up the wall. In 2025, full-height tiling — from floor to ceiling — is the standard for modern bathrooms. This approach creates a seamless, luxurious look while also providing superior moisture protection for the entire room.

Using the same tile on both the floor and walls (or a complementary variation) is a popular technique that makes the bathroom feel unified and well-designed.

6. Bold Pattern Tiles

Geometric patterns, encaustic-look tiles, and bold graphic designs are making a comeback — especially as floor features or accent walls. Hexagons, chevrons, and Moroccan-inspired motifs add personality to bathrooms without overwhelming the space.

The key is to balance busy patterns with neutral surroundings. A patterned floor paired with simple white walls, for example, creates a striking focal point that still feels clean and refined.
